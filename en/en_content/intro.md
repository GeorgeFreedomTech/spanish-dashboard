# Intro - My Journal

This is my Spanish learning journal. It represents my own system based on gamifying foreign language learning, complete with a roadmap and an achievement tracking layout.

The goal is **level B2** (which is more than enough for me).

| Parameter | Baseline Status (as of June 15, 2026) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Age / Gender** | 45 years / Male |
| **Language Background** | CZ (Native), EN (C1) |
| **Spanish - Self-study** | 16 lessons (textbook *Španělština nejen pro samouky* – LEDA) |
| **Spanish - Language Schools** | 2 weeks in Lima (*PeruSpanish* and *Peruwayna*) |
| **Starting Level** | 300–500 words (**A1**); basic grammar (sentence structure, singular/plural, present tense); I can introduce myself and talk about who I am. |

The advantage is that as a native Czech speaker, pronunciation isn't an issue for me. The grammar is also somewhat structurally familiar—verbs change form based on person and tense, while articles and adjectives reflect singular and plural numbers. As an adult, **I can plan my studies and approach them systematically**.

The downside of excessive drilling and a rigid approach is that **it stops being fun** and quickly becomes a boring chore. How to beat this: combine adult tactical planning with a child-like sense of play, effectively blending active and passive learning.

From my experience in programming, weightlifting, and self-defense training, I know that the best way to learn anything is to build the right system and keep clean logs/documentation. **And if it's going to be fast and easy, it has to be varied and fun.**

Right now, I have an overview of basic Spanish grammar and know about 300–500 words (**A1**). But I need to start speaking and automate common phrases and conversations (meaning more than just "Buenos dias" and "Muchas Gracias"). And I want to enjoy the process.

So, I will build exactly that: a **personal system**. I'll map out a **roadmap** and log my progress. This will keep me organized and driven. For every success or milestone achieved, I'll also unlock a reward = **gamification**.

## The System
I will study every single day. If it's really not possible due to external factors, I will at least do some passive listening or a few minutes of vocabulary review... just keep the chain unbroken.
- **Logs:** Every day I will log an entry into this journal (learning documentation).
- **Evaluation:** Every week I will evaluate what worked, what went well, and what I'm struggling with, tweaking and improving my **SYSTEM** as needed.

| Learning Pillar | Strategy & Tools | Operational Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 🔄 **Review** | Vocabulary, phrases, and grammatical patterns | Consolidating, internalizing, and achieving full automation without thinking. |
| 🎧 **Listening** *(High Priority)* | Protocol: 10 min without subtitles + 10 min with subtitles.<br>• TV Series: *Pituca sin Lucas*<br>• YT: *Simple Spanish Podcast*, *Dreaming Spanish* | Fully absorbing the natural rhythm, melody, and spoken pace of the living language. |
| 🗣️ **Speaking** | Active self-talk and simulated conversations (describing reality: what I see, what I am doing, plans for tomorrow, reflection on yesterday). | Unlocking speech mechanics, breaking mental blocks, and testing theory in practice. |
| 🚀 **New Material** | Structured intake of new vocabulary, grammar, and idioms | Gradual, controlled expansion of my active word base. |
| 📝 **Reading + Writing** *(Low Priority)* | Passive complementary activities | Postponed to a later stage (transitioning from A2 to B1). |
